# Credit_Card_Financial_Dashboard
power bi dashboard

Objective
To analyze credit card customer behavior and transaction patterns across demographics, geographies, job types, education, and card categories — enabling the business to understand revenue drivers, customer segments, and usage trends.


Steps:
The analysis is split across two dashboards:
1. Credit Card Customer Report — focuses on who the customers are (demographics, income, geography, marital status, dependents, education).
2. Credit Card Transaction Report — focuses on what they spend on (transaction types, card categories, expense types, quarterly trends).
Both share the same KPI header and can be filtered by quarter, gender, card type, and week.


Key Insights
Customer segment — Businessmen are the most valuable segment, generating $17M in revenue (31% of total) and $2.5M in interest. Retention and premium product targeting here would yield the highest ROI.
Card category — Blue cards dominate at $46M, making up 83% of all revenue. Gold ($2M) and Platinum ($1M) are dramatically underutilized, pointing to a major upsell opportunity.
Education — Graduate customers drive $22M, double that of high school customers. Education is a strong predictor of spending — useful for targeting acquisition campaigns.
Payment method — Swipe transactions lead at $35M while online is just $3M (5% of revenue). Digital channel adoption is critically low, which is both a risk and a growth lever.
Geography — Texas, New York, and California are the top states, each contributing $13–14M combined. California shows a female revenue skew, while TX and NY skew male — useful for regional marketing.
Quarterly stability — Revenue is remarkably consistent at ~$14M per quarter (Q4 dips slightly to $13M), with transaction counts steady around 163–167K. No seasonal volatility is apparent.
